# CSE 312: Product Rule (Multiplication Principle)

#Definition If you have a sequential process and want to find the number of possibilities, where step 1 has $n_1$ options, ..., step $k$ has $n_k$ options, then the total number of outcomes is:

$$n_1 \cdot n_2 \cdots n_k$$

## Examples

**Cooking a Meal:** 
If you decide to cook a meal that always consists of one of three types of root vegetable (potato, yam, or taro root), one of two leafy vegetables (kale or bok choy), and one of four fish (tuna, sardine, salmon, or tilapia), the total number of possible meals is:
$$3 \cdot 2 \cdot 4 = 24$$

**Farming Outfits:**
If a farmer has 3 hats, 3 shirts, and 3 pairs of pants, but each specific shirt restricts the pant choice to only 2 options, the total number of outfits is:
- Step 1: 3 choices for hats
- Step 2: 3 choices for shirts
- Step 3: Regardless of which shirt we choose, we have 2 options for pants.
Total outfits $= 3 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 = 18$
