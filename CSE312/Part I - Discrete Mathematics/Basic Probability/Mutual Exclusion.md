# CSE 312: Mutual Exclusion

#Definition **Mutually exclusive** events cannot occur simultaneously. In notation, $E \cap F = \emptyset$ (i.e., they are disjoint subsets of the sample space). Therefore, their intersection probability is $\mathbb{P}(E \cap F) = 0$.

If $E$ and $F$ are mutually exclusive, then the probability of their union is the sum of their individual probabilities:
$$\mathbb{P}(E \cup F) = \mathbb{P}(E) + \mathbb{P}(F)$$
