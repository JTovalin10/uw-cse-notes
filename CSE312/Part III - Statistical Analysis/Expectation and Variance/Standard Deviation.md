# CSE 312: Standard Deviation

#Definition The **standard deviation** is found by $\sqrt{\sigma^2}$, which is the square root of the [[Variance|variance]]. If we want to find the total standard deviation of $n$ items we simply do $\sqrt{n \cdot \sigma^2}$.

Taking the square root puts the spread back into the same units as $X$ itself, which is why standard deviation is more directly interpretable than variance.
