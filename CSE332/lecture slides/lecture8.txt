AVL Tree
‚ÄĘ A Binary Search tree that maintains that the left and right subtrees of
every node have heights that differ by at most one.
‚ÄĘ height of left subtree and height of right subtree off by at most 1
‚ÄĘ Not too weak (ensures trees are short)
‚ÄĘ Not too strong (works for any number of nodes)

‚ÄĘ Idea of AVL Tree:
‚ÄĘ When you insert/delete nodes, if tree is ‚Äúout of balance‚ÄĚ then modify the tree
‚ÄĘ Modification = ‚Äúrotation‚ÄĚ

Insert Summary
‚ÄĘ After a BST insertion, update the heights of the node‚Äôs ancestors
‚ÄĘ From leaf to root, check if each node is balanced
‚ÄĘ If a node is unbalanced then at the deepest unbalanced node:
‚ÄĘ Case LL: If we inserted in the left subtree of the left child then: rotate right
‚ÄĘ Case RR: If we inserted in the right subtree of the right child then: rotate left
‚ÄĘ Case LR: If we inserted into the right subtree of the left child then: rotate left at
the left child and then rotate right at the root
‚ÄĘ Case RL: If we inserted into the left subtree of the right child then: rotate right at
the right child and then rotate left at the root

‚ÄĘ Done after either reaching the root or applying one of the above cases

Delete Summary
‚ÄĘ Tldr: same cases, reverse direction of rotation, may need to repeat with
ancestors
‚ÄĘ After a BST deletion, update the heights of the node‚Äôs ancestors
‚ÄĘ From leaf to root, check if each node is unbalanced
‚ÄĘ If a node is unbalanced then at the deepest unbalanced node:

‚ÄĘ Case LL: If we deleted in the left subtree of the left child then: rotate left
‚ÄĘ Case RR: If we deleted in the right subtree of the right child then: rotate right
‚ÄĘ Case LR: If we deleted into the right subtree of the left child then: rotate right at the
left child and then rotate left at the root
‚ÄĘ Case RL: If we deleted into the left subtree of the right child then: rotate left at the
right child and then rotate right at the root

‚ÄĘ Continue checking until reach the root
