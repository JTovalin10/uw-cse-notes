# Disk Drives

Disk drives are the primary devices used for [[Secondary Storage]]. They provide non-volatile storage for large amounts of data.

## Main Types

### 1. [[Magnetic Disks|Hard Disk Drives (HDD)]]
Mechanical devices that store data on rotating magnetic platters.
- **Pros**: Low cost per gigabyte, high capacity.
- **Cons**: Slower, susceptible to mechanical failure, performance degrades with fragmentation.

### 2. [[Flash Storage|Solid State Drives (SSD)]]
Electronic devices that store data in semiconductor flash memory.
- **Pros**: Very fast (especially random I/O), silent, durable, low power.
- **Cons**: Higher cost per gigabyte, limited write endurance.

## Key Differences

| Feature | HDD | SSD |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Speed** | Slow (Mechanical) | Fast (Electronic) |
| **Seek Time** | High (~10ms) | Negligible |
| **Durability** | Fragile (Moving parts) | Durable |
| **Life Span** | Decades (if idle) | Limited by write cycles |
