# CSE 312: Permutations

**$P(n,k)$** counts the number of ordered arrangements of $k$ items chosen from $n$ distinct items.

$$P(n, k) = \frac{n!}{(n-k)!}$$

## Industry Standard Terms

- **Permutation** $P(n,k)$ → "$k$-permutation of $n$" / "partial permutation" / "arrangement" (also written $nPk$ or $(n)_k$, the falling factorial).
