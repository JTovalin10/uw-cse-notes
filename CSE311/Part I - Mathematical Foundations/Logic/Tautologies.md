# CSE 311: Tautologies, Contradictions, and Contingencies

#Definition **Tautology**: a proposition that is always true, regardless of the truth values of its variables.

#Definition **Contradiction**: a proposition that is always false, regardless of the truth values of its variables.

#Definition **Contingency**: a proposition that is neither a tautology nor a contradiction — it can be true or false depending on the variable values.
