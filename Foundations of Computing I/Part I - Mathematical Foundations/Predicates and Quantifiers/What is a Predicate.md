# CSE 311: Predicates

#Definition A **predicate** is a [[Foundations of Computing I/Part I - Mathematical Foundations/Functions/Functions|function]] that returns a truth value. Unlike a [[Proposition|proposition]], a predicate has no truth value until its variable is filled in with a specific element.
- Example: $Cat(x) := x \text{ is a cat}$

#Definition The **domain of discourse** is the domain we are working over (e.g., animals, integers, rationals, etc.) — it fixes which elements $x$ may range over.

A predicate becomes a full proposition either by substituting a concrete value for $x$ or by attaching a [[What are Quantifiers|quantifier]].
