#Definition Modulo: $a, b, m \in \mathbb{Z}: m > 0(a \equiv _{m} b)$
Modulo is like a clock where whenever b reaches m it becomes 0
By the definition of congruence $$a \equiv _{m} b \quad \Rightarrow m \mid a-b \quad \text{Definition of congruence}$$
Further, by definition of divides this means
$$
m \mid a-b \quad \Rightarrow a-b = mk, \quad \exists k \in \mathbb{Z}
$$
**Note**: When b is negative we add {m} n times until its positive
ex:
$$
5 \equiv _3 -8 \quad \Rightarrow \quad 5\equiv _3 -8+3k \quad \text{In this case k is 3 which results in: } 5 \equiv _3 1
$$
