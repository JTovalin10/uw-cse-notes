# CSE470: Computer Systems Architecture II

Building upon [[CSE469 Index|CSE469]], this course delves deeper into the advanced micro-architectural techniques used in modern processors, focusing on parallelism, memory consistency, and specialized hardware.

## Topics Covered
- **[[Basic Principles and Performance|Basic Principles and Performance]]**: Moore's Law, Power Wall, and Quantitative Metrics.
- **[[Classes I didnt take/Computer Architecture II/Instruction Set Architecture|Instruction Set Architecture]]**: The challenges of wide-issue front ends.
- **[[Dynamic Scheduling and OoO|Dynamic Scheduling and OoO]]**: Scoreboarding, Tomasulo's Algorithm, and the Reorder Buffer (ROB).
- **[[Memory Hierarchy and Coherence|Memory Hierarchy and Coherence]]**: Cache coherence protocols and multi-porting.
- **[[Memory Consistency and Synchronization|Memory Consistency and Synchronization]]**: Sequential Consistency vs. Relaxed models and atomic primitives.
- **[[Advanced Topics|Advanced Topics]]**: Specialization, Accelerators, and Case Studies (Xbox, Tesla FSD).
