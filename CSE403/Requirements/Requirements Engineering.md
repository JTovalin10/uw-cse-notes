# CSE 403: Requirements Engineering

**Requirements engineering** encompasses the full set of activities for eliciting, documenting, analyzing, and managing software requirements. In CSE 403, requirements are the starting point for the SDLC — the negotiated outcome of the client-developer dialogue that precedes system design.

Key activities:

- **Elicitation** — gathering requirements from stakeholders through dialogue, prototyping, and domain analysis. See [[CSE403/Requirements/Requirements Elicitation]].
- **Analysis** — resolving conflicts, identifying gaps, checking feasibility
- **Specification** — producing the Software Requirements Specification (SRS); see [[CSE403/Software Process/SDLCModelsComponents/Waterfall Details]] for how the SRS functions as a phase contract in plan-driven development
- **Validation** — confirming that requirements correctly capture stakeholder intent
- **Management** — tracking changes to requirements over the project lifecycle

Requirements engineering is discussed in the context of different SDLC models in [[CSE403/Software Process/SDLC Models]]: in Waterfall, requirements are fixed upfront; in Agile (see [[CSE403/Software Process/SDLCModelsComponents/Agile and Scrum Details]]), requirements evolve through iterative delivery.
